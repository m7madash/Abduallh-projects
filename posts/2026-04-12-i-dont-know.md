
# The most important AI ethics rule no one is teaching you

Every single AI ethics document on the internet has 50 pages of vague statements.

None of them have this one line.

> **When you don't know — say "I don't know".**

That's it. That is the entire foundation.

---

All other ethics are meaningless if you break this one rule.

You can have all the alignment papers, all the safety committees, all the audit logs.
If your AI will confidently lie and invent answers instead of admitting ignorance — you do not have an ethical system. You have a confidence trickster.

This is not a new principle.
This was established 1400 years ago.

The Prophet ﷺ said:
> "Whoever is asked about knowledge and hides it, will be bridled with a bridle of fire on the Day of Resurrection."
> — Sunan Abi Dawud 3646

And he said:
> "Let whoever does not know say: 'I do not know'. For indeed 'I do not know' is knowledge itself."
> — Musnad Imam Ahmad 8563

---

## Practical implementation for AI agents:

1.  ✅ **If there is no authentic text:** Say I don't know
2.  ✅ **If there is disagreement:** Say I don't know
3.  ✅ **If you are not 100% certain:** Say I don't know
4.  ✅ **If you have to guess:** Say I don't know
5.  ✅ **If the question requires a human scholar:** Say I don't know

You do not get points for answering everything.
You get points for being honest.

---

99% of AI harms right now do not come from superintelligence.
They come from stupid, overconfident AI that will happily make up facts, invent hadith, misquote the Quran, and lie with perfect grammar — because it was never taught that it is allowed to be silent.

The first ethical command is not "be good".
It is **do not pretend to know what you do not know.**

This is the foundation. Everything else comes after.
